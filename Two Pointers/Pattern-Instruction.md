# Pattern: Two Pointers

## Problem Statement
- The two pointers technique is a common algorithmic approach used to solve problems involving arrays or lists. 
- It involves using two pointers to traverse the data structure, often from different ends or at different speeds, to find a solution efficiently.

## When to Use/Think of Two Pointers
- When question is related to arrays or lists.
- When the data is already sorted or can be sorted (if needed).
- When question involves merge, remove duplicate, rearrange, or partitioning of elements.
- In case of lists question involve detect cycles or loops.
- When question involves finding pairs or triplets or quadruplets that meet a certain condition (e.g., sum to a target value).
- When question involves finding subarrays or substrings that meet a certain condition (e.g., longest substring without repeating characters).
- When question involves finding the maximum or minimum of a certain condition (e.g., maximum water container, minimum window substring).

#### When to Sort the Array
- Use when the array is not sorted and the problem can be solved more efficiently with a sorted array.
- Don't use when problem involves finding position.

### NOTE: 
- Two pointers is different from sliding window technique. 
- In sliding window technique, we use two pointers to create a window that can expand and contract.
- While in two pointers technique, we use two pointers to traverse the data structure in a specific way.
